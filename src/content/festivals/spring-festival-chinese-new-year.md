## The World's Largest Human Migration

Every year, approximately **2 billion trips** are made across China in the 40 days surrounding Chinese New Year. This migration — called **Chunyun** (春运, "spring transport") — is the largest annual human movement on Earth. Train stations become seas of people. High-speed rails add midnight runs. The government deploys temporary staff and military support.

Why? Because for 1.4 billion Chinese people, there is one non-negotiable rule of Spring Festival: **you must be home**. A factory worker in Shenzhen will ride 30 hours standing in a train to reach a village in Henan. A programmer in Beijing will brave gridlock on the expressway to reach parents in Sichuan. Nothing — not cost, not comfort, not a pandemic — overrides this imperative.

## The Legend of Nian

The festival's origin story explains everything about its rituals. **Nian** (年) was a mythical beast that emerged from the mountains every New Year's Eve to devour villagers. One year, an old man discovered Nian's three weaknesses:

1. **Red** — Nian was terrified of the color red
2. **Loud noise** — firecrackers drove it away
3. **Light** — bright illumination repelled it

The villagers survived by pasting red paper on doors, lighting firecrackers, and keeping every lamp burning through the night. The next morning, they congratulated each other on surviving — the original "New Year greeting" (拜年). The beast was never seen again, but the rituals remain, 4,000 years later.

## 15 Days, 15 Rules

Spring Festival is not one day. It is a **15-day observance** with specific customs for nearly each day:

- **Day 0 (New Year's Eve)** — Family reunion dinner (年夜饭), the year's most important meal. Every dish carries symbolism: fish (鱼) for surplus, dumplings (饺子) shaped like gold ingots, sticky rice cake (年糕) for "higher each year."
- **Day 1** — Visit elders, give **red envelopes** (红包, hongbao) with cash to children. No cleaning — you might sweep away luck.
- **Day 2** — Married daughters visit their parents. The only day they are "allowed" to do so.
- **Day 5** — Welcome the **God of Wealth** (财神). Businesses set off the loudest firecrackers of the year.
- **Day 7** — **Renri** (人日), "Everyone's Birthday." All humans add one year.
- **Day 15 (Lantern Festival)** — The grand finale. Red lanterns fill the streets, tangyuan (sweet rice balls) are eaten, and the holiday formally ends.

## The Red Envelope Economy

Red envelopes are not just gifts — they are a **social accounting system**. The amount must be even (odd amounts are for funerals). The bills must be crisp new currency. And there is a subtle reciprocity: if someone gave your child 200 yuan last year, you give their child 200 yuan this year. Over a lifetime, hongbao exchanges between families roughly balance out — making the system self-sustaining without impoverishing anyone.

In the digital age, WeChat and Alipay have replaced physical envelopes. During the 2024 Spring Festival, **over 40 billion digital hongbao** were sent via WeChat alone. But grandparents still prefer the paper version — because the act of pressing a red envelope into a child's hand carries something no app can transmit.
