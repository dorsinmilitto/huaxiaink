The guzheng (古筝) has been strummed for over 2,500 years, but when Mojin plays modern tracks on this ancient instrument, something magical happens — past and present dissolve into pure sound.

## The Instrument

The guzheng is a 21-string plucked zither that dates back to the Warring States period (475–221 BC). Its sound is instantly recognizable: cascading bends, shimmering harmonics, and a resonance that seems to hang in the air forever.

## The Revival

What you're witnessing isn't just a cover — it's part of a movement. Across China, young musicians are picking up traditional instruments and playing everything from anime soundtracks to hip-hop. The hashtag #国风 (Guofeng, "national style") has billions of views on Chinese social media, signaling a generational shift: young Chinese aren't leaving tradition behind — they're reinventing it.

Artists like Mojin represent this new wave. Classically trained, but unafraid to cross boundaries. The result? Music that bridges dynasties.
